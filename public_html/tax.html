The tax residence in Rome

From the date of September 1, 2014 the City Council of Rome introduces increased tax residence for tourists who stay in the city of Rome (Comune di Roma) in officially registered hotel facilities such as campsites, apartments, B & B, houses pilgrim and holiday homes, hotels and residences. So you have this duty to call more tax room, because tourists spending the night in private homes are not required to pay it.

Tax is paid in principle directly at the reception of the hotel. Most of the hotels taking reservations online and travel agencies issuing voucher booked by hotels do not add on the tax to the price of accommodation. So booking accommodation in Rome should always ask whether the tax residence has been included ...

FEES since 01/09/2014


Youth hostels - are not subject to excise residence permit
Camping, camping, camper field = 2 euro / per person / per night for the first 5 days of accommodation.
Agritourism = 4 euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
B & B = 3.5 Euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Apartments and rooms for rent = 3.5 Euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Houses pilgrim and holiday homes (casa per ferie) = 3.5 Euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Pensions = 4 euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Residence = 4 euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Hotels 1-2 stars = 3 euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Hotels 3 stars = 4 euro / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Hotels 4 stars = 6 euros / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.
Hotels 5 = 7 euros / per person / per night for the first 10 days of accommodation.

EXEMPTIONS FROM TAX RESIDENCE PERMIT


They are exempted from paying tax:
a) children up to and including ten years;
b) those who stay at hostels.
c) The sick and those who assist patients admitted to hospitals (in a ratio of 1 tutor for 1 patient).
d) the parents or guardians of underage patients in hospitals of Rome (required here is to provide a certificate from the hospital confirming the identity of the patient and the period of hospitalization).
e) tourist coach drivers and tour guides who stay with tourist groups (ratio 1 free every 23 participants).
