The Dome of St. Peter's Basilica

The dome of St. Peter's Basilica. Peter was designed by Michelangelo Buonarroti (1475-1564). Its construction was interrupted after his death in 1564 at the height of the drum. The work was taken up again by Sixtus V (1585-1590) by Giacomo della Porta (1533-1602) and Domenico Fontana (1543-1607). It started in 1588 and continued with the involvement of approx. 800 employees. After twenty-two months of continuous operation in the summer of 1590 the work could be regarded as completed and the Mass was celebrated Thanksgiving. During the pontificate of Clement VIII (1592-1605) completed the construction of the dome with a lantern and was covered with sheet lead. On 18 November 1593 at the top of the lighthouse they placed a great ball, and on the gilded bronze cross, made by Sebastiano Torrigiani.
