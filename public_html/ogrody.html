Vatican Gardens

Gardens of the Pope in the Vatican, full of different species of trees, shrubs, flowers and herbs, are a major tourist attraction for people who love nature. They are indeed the largest such attraction in Rome, where parks and botanical gardens missing, but their unfeigned charm is its proximity important for many people, places and people: the tomb of St. Peter, the masterpieces of the Vatican Museums, and living near the popes ...
The current form of the Vatican Gardens is the result of many years of change, ideas and work done during the pontificate of various popes. The origins of the park dates back to the thirteenth century.
The gardens can be visited on foot, but only in groups with a guide Vatican or from the deck minibus objeżdżającego most important avenues papal park.
Tickets can be purchased only on line at the Vatican Museums: - Ordinary 31 euro (includes admission to the Museum, the Sistine Chapel and gardens) - Reduced 25 euros (the conditions for relief).
Reservations for groups of tickets to be made by fax (+39 0669885100) no earlier than 2 months and not later than 2 weeks before the planned visit. In response obtained voucher (confirmation of booking tickets), which should appear on a day trip in the "Visite guidate" Vatican Museums, no later than 15 minutes before the start of sightseeing. In the absence of a confirmed booking, you can not purchase a ticket.
