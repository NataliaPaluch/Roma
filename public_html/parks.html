Rome - Natural Parks


Rome is crowded and loud every day. It offers tourists the opportunity to see numerous monuments, works of art and antique memorabilia. It's a beautiful place, but unfortunately it is always crowded and noisy. Therefore, although it is worth a moment to breathe, catch a breath and relax away from the hustle and bustle. The best place is undoubtedly the natural parks and nature reserves, which in Rome, luckily missing.

Marcigliana


Reserve covers an area of ​​4696 hectares in the hills of the Tiber bounded to the west, south ditch Bufalotta, and from the north of Rio del Casale, which marks the administrative boundaries of the city. On the one hand, the gently rounded hills today grown various crops (eg. Sunflowers), while on the other side of the hill are the remains of the oak forest, mixed with maple and hornbeam. Fauna species living in the park include foxes, martens, weasels, hedgehogs and badgers and even species found nowhere else hares. The picturesque landscapes of this park are normally part are old, standing alone rural houses, often built on the remains of the former Roman estates and medieval towers. On one of the hills of the park found the ruins of the ancient city Crustumerium, once an important checkpoint on the road between Etruria and Campania.

Aguzzano


Park, founded in 1989, located between Via Nomentana, Via Tiburtina and Rome ring road (GRA), the green lung of Rome. It lies in the densely populated neighborhoods and at the same time is the most-loved meeting place of Romans living in the eastern part of the city. A characteristic feature of this park are the rows of tall, slender trees, forming charming alleys: plane trees, pines and poplars.

VALLE Dell'Aniene


Numerous twists and turns of the river Aniene river, which originates in the mountains Simbruini and after approx. 120 km flows into the Tiber in northern Rome, are part of the inscribed in the architectural structure of the city. They intersect most densely populated, the eastern districts and run towards the ring road (GRA). It all takes place in the park Valle Dell'Aniene. The element that distinguishes the nature reserve from others is almost flat, large area, where there are sources of Acqua Vergine and the area Pantano Borghese, which is also home to many species of fauna and flora. The waters of the river they live crayfish, and in the trees and numerous corners of the park colonies of bats. Most frequently occurring species of plants in the area are oaks, maples, birch and ash. There is a significant historic Ponte Nomentano, which probably dates from the era of Agrippa; whole, along with the medieval and revival superstructure, was the object of interest of the painters of different eras.

DECIMA - Malafede


Natural nature reserve Decima - Malafede is the largest park complex protected by RomaNatura (the organization that manages the natural protected areas in Rome). On the territory of this park are the largest forested area of ​​Agro Romano. The park is located between the Roman ring road GRA, via Pontina, Via Laurentina and the neighboring municipality of Pomezia. It is interesting that perhaps these areas were the first to settle, approx. 250,000 years ago. In the days of the empire they created numerous villas, which then, in the Middle Ages, has been transformed into large farms, fortresses and towers, fulfilling the role of checkpoints in the territory. Park covers an area of ​​6145 hectares.

Laurentino - ACQUA acetosa


It's tiny, natural nature reserve, located in the south-west of Rome, north buildings restricted buildings densely populated district Laurentino. It is located in the valley, which is also ditch Acqua Acetosa - a source of mineral water. In the area of ​​the park was discovered a large necropolis, a remnant of the old town conquered by the Romans. Interesting and valuable artifacts found in the necropolis can be seen today in the National Museum in Rome. In the park you can walk between the pillars of eucalyptus, planted here in the nineteenth century (when it was thought that the balsamic aroma of this plant helps combat various diseases). You can also see a basalt quarry near Casali di S.Sisto.
