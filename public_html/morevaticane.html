Vatican City

As is well known, the State of Vatican City (Stato della Citta Vaticana) is the smallest country in the world. Its official boundaries encompass only 0.44 kmÂ² right bank of Rome, on the hill dedicated to the ancient cult of the Etruscan god Vaticanus. Obviously so small, and yet so important to the state, it would not be able to function well without a number of other properties located on the territory of the Italian Republic and enjoying the privilege of extraterritoriality, such as the basilica patriarchal palaces of several congregations and offices papal several large medical facilities and care , including the famous hospitals: Bambino Gesu (children) and the university clinic Gemelli. If still we add to this residence of the Pope in Castel Gandolfo, much bigger than the Vatican itself (approx. 55 hectares) and hundreds of properties belonging to religious orders, religious congregations and parishes, we understand that the material and the spiritual Vatican is much larger than indicated by the official boundaries.
