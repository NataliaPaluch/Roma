Pantheon - the Temple of all the gods

Pantheon is a majestic temple and one of the best preserved ancient monuments in Europe. This powerful design takes its name from the Greek words pan - and all theoi - gods and was built just to honor the most important Roman deities. At the beginning of the seventh century has been adapted to the Christian church of Santa Maria ad Martyres - Blessed Mary of the Martyrs.

The circular structure was built on the Champ de Mars, at the behest of the emperor Hadrian in the years 118 - 125 r. Century AD, on the site of an earlier temple built a century earlier during the reign of Augustus. On the facade of the Pantheon bears a large inscription in bronze: M.AGRIPPA.LFCOS.TERTIUM.FECIT (it was build by Marcus Agrippa, son of Lucius, Consul for the third time), with the result that the builder of the temple was just a friend and son-in-Octavian Augustus, Marek Agrippa.

For the interior of the temple have large, 20-tone original doors cast in bronze.

Pantheon is housed in a small, charming square Piazza della Rotonda, full of cafes and restaurants. You can very well spend the afternoon with coffee, enjoying the wonderful view of this amazing monument of Rome which is the Pantheon.

Entrance to the Pantheon is so far free of charge; building is sometimes closed on Sunday mornings (masses).
