Cycling in Rome

A tourist in Rome has a lot of possibilities for movement. Available are buses, metro, trams, trolley buses, or taxis. But there are places where the subway does not reach (the old part of Rome), taxis and buses are standing in traffic jams, and the trams are very crowded. Looking at the daily kilometer traffic jams on the busy streets of Rome has the urge to jump on the tiny skuterek, who boldly flits between the cars. Unfortunately, even scooters can not drive anywhere.

Therefore, the best solution is bicycles!

They are in the Eternal City an advantage even over the scooters! There are places where guests can access only pedestrians and cyclists (some streets of Trastevere). Very easy to flit between historical buildings, huge squares, cobbled narrow streets and alleys asphalt. Cyclists on the streets of Rome are becoming more common view, a tour of Rome in this way, it has now become surprisingly easy.

In the city there are many cycle hire, finally started service bike sharing, and there are numerous trails and bike paths, and city authorities from time to time shall prohibit traffic for motor vehicles. See you on the way!

Cycling Routes


TEVERE NORD

Castel Giubileo - P.le Maresciallo Giardino- Ponte risorgimento (15 Km)
For the first 12 km it is going to shaft the river, while the remaining 3 km run through the city; takes you to Ponte Risorgimento.
Viale Angelico (1 Km)
The route connects the trail Tevere Nord district of Prati. It was built on the occasion of the World Cup in 1990.
Viale Angelico-Piazza Cavour
The route starting on Viale Angelico, runs Viale Giulio Cesare, Via Lepanto, Via Marcantonio Colonna, until Piazza Cavour.

DUE VILLE

Lungotevere della Vittoria - Ponte Risorgimento - Villa Borghese - Villa Ada (4 Km)
The route runs from Piazza Risorgimento, the Viale delle Belle Arti, Via Rossini, Piazza Ungheria and Via Panama. It combines the Parco Fluviale del Tevere from the Villa Borghese and Villa Ada.

CICLOVIA DELLA MUSICA

Ponte Milvio- Auditorium-Villa Glori-Villa Ada (Via Salaria)

ANIENE

Villa Ada (Via Salaria) - Ponte Nomentano (Via Valsolda) 4.2 Km
