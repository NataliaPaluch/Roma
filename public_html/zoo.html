Roman Zoo

An excellent idea, especially for tourists with children, is undoubtedly a visit to Bioparco - Rome zoo. Where nature becomes culture.

Thousands of species of animals, educational meetings, games and fun in the air and in halls, restaurants and places for picnics ... everything closed on 18 hectares in the heart of the park of Villa Borghese - this is Roman zoo. Every year is visited by more than half a million people, of which approx. 50,000 students. This alternative to traditional destinations, which are museums, monuments and churches.

In the zoo there are many species of animals, both well-known and popular as well as less common: Croatian wolves, chimpanzees, ostriches, antelopes, zebras, Asiatic lions, South American tapirs, capybaras, rheas, flamingos, turtles and many others.

There are organized numerous demonstrations, events, performances and performances involving animals and animal. It is a perfect place for both children and adults, where you can get and deepen the ecological knowledge, learn how to help animals, protect them and promote the protection of endangered species.

For children there is a special dedicated area where they can directly associate with various animals, other commonly encountered. It is also possible to organize a birthday party for children.

Zoo is located in the Villa Borghese, Viale del Giardino Zoologico, 20. http://www.bioparco.it

Open daily from. 9: 30-17: 00 (January 1 - March 28) 9: 30-18: 00 (March 29 - October 26), 9: 30-17: 00 (October 27 - December 31). Extended opening hours (9:30 - 19:00) are on Saturdays, Sundays and public holidays during the period 5 April - 28 September.
